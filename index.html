The Times & Sunday Times Election Data

Visualizing the projected results by parliamentary constituency.

Overall Vote Share by Party

Labour leads with 38.7% of the total votes, making it the most popular party in this election. Conservatives and Reform each have 15.5%, showing substantial support. The Liberal Democrats secured 11.7%, while the Green Party has 6.7%. The Scottish National Party (SNP) garnered 3.1%, with Plaid Cymru and others receiving 0.7% and 1.9% respectively.

Number of Constituencies Won by Party

The Labour Party has secured a significant majority with 425 constituencies, making it the dominant party in this election. The Conservatives follow with 108 constituencies, which is significantly fewer than Labour, indicating a major shift in voter preference compared to previous elections. The Liberal Democrats have won 67 constituencies, showing a notable presence but still far behind Labour and Conservatives. The Scottish National Party has secured 20 constituencies, reflecting its stronghold in Scotland. Reform has won 5 constituencies, and Plaid Cymru has won 4, indicating limited but specific regional support. The Green Party has secured 2 constituencies, showing a small yet significant presence.

Correlation of parties with different regions

This heatmap visualizes the correlation of different political parties with various regions. Focusing on the Labour Party, we can observe that it has the highest correlation in regions such as Yorkshire and The Humber, London, North West, and North East, all showing a correlation of 0.5. This indicates that Labour has a strong presence and significant support in these regions. On the other hand, Labour has a moderate correlation in regions like West Midlands and East Midlands, with a correlation of 0.4. The data suggests that the Labour Party's influence is more pronounced in certain regions, which could be strategic areas for their campaign focus.
